Modern Software Tools, Explained

The concepts behind the tools programmers use every day — how they work, why they are designed the way they are, and what tradeoffs they make.

Part One

Data Structures

The shapes data takes in memory — and what those shapes make fast or slow.

  • Arrays Think in Arrays, Not in Loops Rank, shape, broadcasting, reduce, and tacit composition from APL, in runnable JavaScript.
  • Text Buffers Ropes and Piece Tables The text-buffer structures behind modern editors: gap buffers, ropes, piece tables, and undo history.
  • Trees Filesystems and B-Trees Why B-trees matter for disk-backed systems, including search, splitting, deletion, and a miniature filesystem.
  • Balanced Trees The Balanced Bookshelf AVL trees, red-black trees, ordered maps, and when a balanced tree beats a hash table.
  • Skip Lists The Casino Data Structure Probabilistic balancing, Redis sorted sets, rank queries, and a full skip-list implementation.
  • Approximation Good Enough Answers Bloom filters, count-min sketch, HyperLogLog, and the case for approximate answers.
Part Two

Storage Engines

How data survives a crash, scales past memory, and stays consistent under concurrency.

  • LSM Trees The Write-Heavy Problem MemTables, SSTables, compaction, Bloom filters, and the tradeoffs behind LevelDB and RocksDB.
  • Transactions Transaction Control from First Principles Isolation, MVCC, write-ahead logging, and distributed commit as one coherent story.
Part Three

Algorithms

Sorting, searching, and compressing — the workhorses underneath every system.

  • Sorting The Practical Champion Quicksort, pivot choice, introsort, and the cases where the fastest in-memory sorter is not the right tool.
  • External Sorting Sorting Bigger Than RAM External mergesort, run generation, k-way merging, and sequential I/O.
  • Compression The Compression Game Shannon entropy, Huffman coding, LZ77 back-references, and how gzip combines both.
  • Image Compression How JPEG Works Color transforms, chroma subsampling, DCT, quantisation, and the block-level mechanics of lossy compression.

Networks and Security

Reliability, trust, and secrecy across an untrusted network.

  • TCP/IP The Reliable Network How IP routes around failure and how TCP repairs loss with ACKs and retransmission.
  • Cryptography The Two-Key Trick Public-key cryptography, toy RSA, signatures, and the role these ideas play in TLS.
  • TLS How TLS Secures the Internet The TLS 1.3 handshake, Diffie-Hellman, certificates, and forward secrecy.

  • Languages Two Languages That Broke the Rules Lisp and Forth: homoiconicity, eval, closures, macros, stacks, and malleable language design.
