The Infinite Tablet

Mathematical ideas from Sumer to Gödel — tracing how mathematics grew from practical problems into abstract thought.

From clay tablet to incompleteness
  • 3000–500 BCE Babylon & Egypt — accounting, geometry, proof
  • 600–200 BCE Greece — Euclid, Archimedes, the dangerous idea of proof
  • 400–1400 CE India & Islam — zero, algebra, the Kerala school
  • 1600–1800 Europe — calculus, probability, complex numbers
  • 1800–1900 Modernity — non-Euclidean space, symmetry, infinity
  • 1900–1930 The limits — relativity, Cantor, Gödel
